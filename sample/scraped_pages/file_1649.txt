French Cultural Institute Madrasset Al-Huquq Al Frinseya St, Mounira, Tel 02 2791 5800 To celebrate the end of a year marked by the Paris 2024 Olympic and Paralympic Games, the French Institute in Egypt is organizing a group exhibition. This event will display photographs capturing the highlights of the games, taken by amateurs and professionals in both France and Egypt. The selected images will reflect the memories and emotions associated with this historic sporting event. The exhibition celebrates the intersection of creativity and sporting culture, bringing together diverse perspectives on an unforgettable global experience (12 December- 12 January). Goethe Institute 5 El-Bustan Street, Downtown, Tel 02 2575 9877/ 219/ 01288711134 Takhshena gallery “Ready to Leave” installation, part of Ready to Go project, by Sameh El-Taweel transforms the car into a powerful symbol of migration, identity and displacement. Using a combination of found objects, cultural symbols and soundscapes, El-Taweel delves into the emotional tension between the desire to move and the inability to let go of the past (closing 15 December). Naguib Mahfouz Museum Tekkeyet Mohamed Bek Abul-Dahab, next to Al-Azhar Mosque, Al-Darb Al-Ahmar, Tel 0122 723 1573 “Tagore - Mahfouz Caricature” exhibition, organised by Naguib Mahfouz Museum, commemorates the birth anniversary of great Egyptian novelist Nobel laureate Naguib Mahfouz, the literary achievements of Indian Nobel laureate, Rabindranath Tagore will also be celebrated. The exhibition presents a collection of caricatures that bring together two of the world’s greats who won the Nobel Prize in Literature. A group of prominent Egyptian cartoonists participate such as Ahmed Alawi , Hosni Abbass , Adham Lotfy , Farouk Moussa , Tharwat Mortada , Ghada Mustafa , Hadeer Yehia , and Hani Abdel Gawad (11- 17 December). Tintera 17 Bahgat Aly St, Apt 14, second floor, Zamalek, Tel 01005655005 Gallery Hours: Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, 11am-5pm Wednesday & Saturday, 11am-7pm Friday closed “Altered Landscapes” third solo exhibition by René Clement . Expanding on his on-going series I Feel The Earth Move, Clement’s two new bodies of work, Fractals Arabesque and White Desert-Black Nights, continue his exploration of the landscape and the visualisation of the passage of time through his use of digital collage and manipulation (11 December- 10 January).
